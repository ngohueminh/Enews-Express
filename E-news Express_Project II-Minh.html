Objective:¶

  • Explore the dataset and extract insights using Exploratory Data Analysis .
  • The overaching question is to help the business determine whether the new landing page is more effective than the old one . To answer that, we will look to answer these questions:
  • 1.Do customers spend more time on the new landing page?
  • 2.Is the conversion rate of the new landing page higher than old landing page?
  • 3.Does the converted status depend on the prefered language?
  • 4.Is the mean time spent on the new page same for the different language users?
  • After we have the insights we can conclude whether the new landing page works

Data:¶

abtest.csv - it contains information about customers purchasing product.

  • user_id - This represents the user ID of the person visiting the website.
  • group - This represents whether the user belongs to the first group (control) or the second group (treatment).
  • landing_page - This represents whether the landing page is new or old.
  • time_spent_on_the_page - This represents the time (in minutes) spent by the user on the landing page.
  • converted - This represents whether the user gets converted to a subscriber of the news portal or not.
  • language_preferred - This represents the language chosen by the user to view the landing page.

Q1 - Do customers spend more time on the new landing page?¶

Visualisation: time spent on new and old landing page¶

In [35]:

Step 1: Define null and alternate hypotheses¶

$H_0:$ mean of time spent on the control group is equal to that of treatment group

$H_a:$ mean of time spent on the control group is smaller than that of treatment group

Let $\mu_1, \mu_2$ be the mean of time_spent_on_the_page of control group and treatment group respectively.

We will test the null hypothesis

$H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2$

against the alternate hypothesis

$H_a:\mu_1<\mu_2$

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

For the problem, we have to compare the sample means from 2 independent population when standard deviation are unknown. Therefore we will use 2-sample independent t-test here

Let's test whether the T-test assumptions are satisfied or not¶

  • Continuous data - Yes, the usage time is measured on a continuous scale.
  • Normally distributed populations - Yes, we can assume the population is normal and sample size n>30.
  • Independent populations - As we are taking random samples for two different type of users, the two samples are from two independent populations.
  • Random sampling from the population - Yes, we are informed that the collected sample is a simple random sample.
  • Population standard deviation is known - No

  • Unequal population standard deviations - As the sample standard deviations are different, the population standard deviations may be assumed to be different.

    We can use 2 sample T-test for this problem: testing the equality of sample means of 2 samples

Step 3: Decide significance level¶

For this test we select α = 0.05.

Step 4: Collect & prepare data¶

print('The p-value is ', p_value)

Insight¶

As the p-value (~0.0001) is less than the level of significance (0.05), we can reject the null hypothesis. Thus, it may be concluded that there is enough statistical evidence to conclude that the time spent on the new landing page (treatment group) is higher than old landing page (control group)

Step 1: Define null and alternate hypotheses¶

$H_0:$ conversion rate of control group is equal to that of treatment group

$H_a:$ conversion rate of control group is smaller than that of treatment group

Let $p_1,p_2$ be the proportions of conversion (conversion ratio) in control group and treatment group respectively.

We will test the null hypothesis

$H_0:p_1 =p_2$

against the alternate hypothesis

$H_a:p_1 < p_2$

Step 2: Select appropriate test¶

With this problem, we need to compare the proportion (conversion rate) of two populations (control and treatment group) and conclude if conversion rate of new landing page is higher than old landing page Therefore we will use the 2 sample 1 tailed Z-test (proportion)

Let's test whether the Z-test assumptions are satisfied or not¶

  • Binomally distributed population - Yes, a conversion option is either CONVERTED or NOT CONVERTED
  • Random sampling from the population - Yes, we are informed that the collected sample is a simple random sample.
  • Can the binomial distribution approximated to normal distribution - Yes.

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

We select α = 0.05.

Step 4: Collect & prepare data¶

  • expected value of the number of sample observations in each level of the variable is at least 5: yes
  • Random sampling from the population: yes, we know that the sample is randomly collected Hence we can use Chi-Square Test for independence

Step 3: Decide significance level¶

For this test we select α = 0.05.

This is a problem concerning 3 population means, One - way Anova could be the appropriate test here provided the normality

Now, the normality and equality of variance assumptions need to be checked.

  • Assumption 1: For testing of normality, Shapiro-Wilk’s test is used
  • Assumption 2: for testing of equality of variance, Levene test is used

Lastly, to answer the question / hypothesis about equality of mean, We will use Tukey HD test.

Shapiro-Wilk’s test¶

We will test the null hypothesis

𝐻0: time spent follows a normal distribution against

against the alternative hypothesis

𝐻𝑎: time spent n does not follow a normal distribution We will use the significance level of 0.05

Levene’s test¶

We will test the null hypothesis
